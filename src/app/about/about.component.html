COVID-19 is an illness caused by a Corona Virus that can spread from one person to another. The virus was first detected in late 2019 in Wuhan, China and has since spread globally. COVID-19 symptoms can range from mild(or no symptoms) to severe illness. Here is what you need to know:

Signs andSymptoms of COVID-19

Not everyone who gets coronavirus has symptoms, but when they do they can range from mild to severe. These are the primary symptoms:

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Headache

Some people may also experience:

  • Body aches
  • Nasal congestion
  • Runny nose
  • Sore throat
  • Diarrohea

If you, or someone you know are experiencing any of the following symptoms, please go to a hospital immediately. You are having a medical emergency.

  • Severe difficulty breathing (e.g., struggling for each breath, speaking in single words)
  • Severe chest pain
  • Confusion (for example, unsure of where you are)
  • Lost consciousness or extreme difficulty waking up
  • A high fever (your temperature is above 38° Celsius/100.4° Fahrenheit)
I am at high risk of complications from COVID-19 if?
  • I am undergoing a medical treatment that suppresses my immune system (for example, HIV/AIDS, cancer/chemotherapy/radiation).
  • I have a chronic health condition (for example, diabetes, high blood pressure, heart condition).
  • I have a respiratory or lung condition.
  • I have been treated for tuberculosis.

If one or more of the above apply to you, take additional precautions to prevent getting infected with COVID-19. Monitor your symptoms carefully and if they become more serious, call the ministry hotlines immediately.

How do i get infected?
  • COVID-19 is primarily spread from person to person via respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.
  • You may also contract COVID-19 by touching a surface or object with the virus on it, and then touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.
How do i protect myself?
  • Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 30 seconds.
  • Clean your hands with hand sanitizer when soap and water are not available.
  • Disinfect surfaces you are frequently in contact with, such as door knobs, tables, light switches, toilets, etc.
  • Maintain two or more meters distance between yourself and anybody else, especially if they show symptoms.
  • Do not shake hands, hug or touch anybody.
  • Avoid large gatherings of people, including sporting events, religious gatherings, parties, etc.
  • Wear a mask when you are outside of your home, or if you are feeling sick.
